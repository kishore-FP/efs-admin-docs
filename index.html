Overview

EFS Admin is the back-office portal for an enterprise facilities-services business. It gives an account manager / operations admin a single place to run the day-to-day of every cleaning contract — from setting up a new site through to approving the payroll at the end of the month.

How to navigate: the Sidebar on the left is the way you move around the app. Checklists and Settings are parent items — click to expand and reveal their sub-pages.
Sidebar map: Facilities · Supply Requests · Issues · Checklists (Recurring Checklists, Audit Checklists, Recurring Jobs, Audit Jobs) · Documents · Payroll · Profitability · Evaluations · Users · Messages · Settings (Evaluation Parameters).

The app is organised around six big things:

  • Facilities — every customer site you service, with its staff, shifts, supplies, contacts and revenue.
  • Work — what's getting done at each site: supply requests, reported issues, recurring cleaning jobs, audits.
  • Templates — the recurring checklists and audit checklists that field staff fill in when they clock on.
  • People — staff users, their teams, and the team chat.
  • Money — payroll approvals and the per-facility profitability dashboard.
  • Quality — staff evaluations and the parameters they're scored against.

The sidebar on the left is the way you move around the app. Checklists and Settings expand to show their sub-pages.

EFS Admin (this web portal) is paired with EFS Mobile — the phone app used by cleaners in the field and by account managers on the go. The two apps share the same database. Most of the data the admin sees here originates on mobile (clock-ins, completed checklist items, issue reports with photos, supply requests) — so a lot of admin work is reviewing and approving things rather than creating them from scratch. The End-to-end Roadmap walks through five complete scenarios covering both apps.

Facilities

A facility is a single customer site — a building, school, office, retail location — where EFS provides cleaning. The Facilities page is the master list of every site under contract.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Facilities
To open one site: click any row in the list to open Facility Detail.

What you see on the Facilities page

Each row shows the facility's name, address, geofence status (Active with a radius in metres, or Inactive), monthly revenue, cleaning frequency and supply responsibility. Stat cards at the top show total facilities, daily-cleaned count, and how many have geofencing turned on.

Creating a new facility

  1. Open Facilities from the sidebar.
  2. Click + Add Facility in the top-right.
  3. Fill in:
    • Facility Name (required), address, city, state, ZIP.
    • Square footage, cleaning frequency, days cleaned, scope notes.
    • Supply responsibility — whether EFS or the client supplies cleaning materials.
    • Geofence — pick a location on the map (sets latitude / longitude) and enter a radius in metres. When set, cleaners can only clock in/out from inside this radius.
  4. Click Create Facility. The new row appears immediately on the list.
Client contacts and monthly revenue are not on the create form — you add them after the fact from the Client Contacts and Monthly Revenue tabs inside Facility Detail. See Flow Details → Facilities for the exact create-form field list.

Inside a facility (the Facility Detail page)

Click any row to open the detail page. The left side has a vertical tab strip with eight tabs — these are the seven things you can configure for one site, plus the headline information panel:

  1. Staff — who's assigned to this site, split between Primary (regular roster) and Backup (cover only). Use + Assign Staff to add someone.
  2. Shift Schedule — the recurring weekly schedule of when cleaners should be on site. Use + Add Shift to create one, or Delete All to wipe and rebuild.
  3. Supplies — log of supplies that have been used / delivered to this site, plus free-form supply notes.
  4. Client Contacts — additional people at the customer beyond the primary contact.
  5. Access & Alarms — entry codes, key locations, alarm instructions, parking notes.
  6. Additional Info — any extra free-text notes you want kept against this site.
  7. Supply Catalog — the list of supply items typically ordered for this facility (used as the picklist when raising a supply request).
  8. Monthly Revenue — month-by-month revenue history with an editable amount per month.

The header strip stays the same as you switch tabs — it always shows the facility name, address, and the Update Location button which opens a popup to change the geofence coordinates.

Editing or deleting a facility

From the Facilities list, the row buttons let you open detail or open the edit form directly. Deleting is soft (the row sets a delete flag, it isn't physically removed) — so deleted facilities won't show up but historical payroll, audit and revenue records still link back to them.

Supply Requests

A supply request is an order for cleaning supplies, PPE or equipment for a specific facility — typically raised by the cleaner or supervisor at the site, and reviewed/processed by an admin.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Supply Requests
To open one request: click any row to open Supply Request Detail. From there, click Download (top-right) to open the printable invoice view.

The supply requests list

Shows every request with: request number, date, facility, requester (and their role), the items asked for, total quantity, total cost, and current status. The stat cards at the top break down counts by status. The toolbar has a Status dropdown (Pending / Ordered / Delivered / Collected), a Facility filter and a date range. The ✕ Clear button resets all filters.

Creating a supply request from the admin side

  1. Open Supply Requests from the sidebar.
  2. Click + New Request (or use the create button on the toolbar).
  3. Pick the facility, the requester, and add line items (item name, category, quantity, unit cost). Subtotal and total compute automatically.
  4. Click Submit. The request lands in the list with status Pending.
Most supply requests are raised from the EFS Mobile app by cleaners on site. The admin's job is usually to process requests, not create them.

Who drives the workflow

The supply request workflow can be driven from both apps — they share the same database. In practice the AM does most of the day-to-day approvals from their phone (because they're often on site), but the web admin's Update Status popup has every status option too, with a dedicated update query per status that stamps the same dates / user ids. The two apps differ only in the reject with reason flow, which lives only on AM mobile.

A rejected request additionally stores rejected_reason.

The web Update Status popup

Click Update Status on any supply request's detail page. The popup ("Choose the new status for this supply request") shows five status cards:

  • Pending Approval — Awaiting confirmation from supplies team
  • Approved — Approved by manager
  • Ordered — Order placed with supplier
  • Delivered — Items received at facility
  • Collected — Picked up by requester

Pick a card → Update Status button at the bottom commits. The popup does not have a Rejected option — to reject a request the AM has to do it from mobile (the reason field is a hard requirement).

Other web actions on the request

  • Edit Request — change high-level fields (facility, requester, notes).
  • + Add Item — add a line item; row inline edit / delete to change qty / cost.
  • Download — open the printable Supply Request Invoice view.
The web list filter only exposes the four most common day-to-day statuses (pending · ordered · delivered · collected) in its dropdown. To see approved or rejected rows specifically, leave the filter blank — the column itself shows the actual value. Both apps see the same data.

Generating an invoice

Click Download on the detail page to open the Supply Request Invoice view — a printable / exportable summary of the request with line items, subtotal, totals and metadata, suitable for sending to the client or accounting.

Issues

An issue is anything reported as wrong at a site — broken equipment, a safety concern, a maintenance need, an access problem. They're typically reported from the EFS Mobile app and reviewed in this admin portal.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Issues
To open one issue: click any row to open Issue Detail. The Update Issue button (top-right of detail) opens a popup for status / resolved-by / notes.

The issues list

Shows: title, status, severity, type, facility, who reported it, where on the site, and date. Stat cards at the top show total open, urgent counts, etc. The toolbar lets you filter by facility, status and severity.

Resolving an issue

  1. Click any row to open Issue Detail.
  2. Read the description, look at any attached photos, and check who reported it.
  3. Click Update Issue to change status — moving it through Open → In Progress → Resolved → Closed as the work happens.
  4. You can also reassign the issue (set Resolved by) and add resolution notes.
  5. Click Update in the popup to save changes.
Severity levels — Low, Medium, High, Critical — drive how the issue is sorted and surfaced. Use Critical sparingly, only for safety problems that need same-day attention.

Recurring Checklists

A recurring checklist is a template of tasks that a cleaner has to complete on a schedule (every shift, weekly, monthly). The cleaner sees these in the EFS Mobile app and ticks items off as they go. From the admin side you build and maintain the templates.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Checklists → Recurring Checklists
From the list: + Create Checklist opens the create form · row eye button opens read-only Checklist Detail · row pencil button opens Edit Checklist.

Creating a checklist

  1. Open Checklists → Recurring Checklists from the sidebar.
  2. Click + Create Checklist.
  3. Give the checklist a name (e.g. Daily Restroom Clean).
  4. Add items one at a time:
    • Item name — the task description.
    • Frequency — Daily, Weekly or Monthly.
    • If Weekly, pick which days of the week the item applies to.
    • If Monthly, pick the day of the month it's due.
  5. Pick which facilities this checklist applies to (multi-select).
  6. Save. The checklist is now visible to cleaners on those facilities in the mobile app.

Editing or deleting a checklist

Click any row's edit button to open the same form pre-filled. The eye button opens a read-only detail view. Delete is soft — old checklist instances that have already been completed by cleaners stay in the audit history.

Audit Checklists

An audit checklist is a one-off inspection template — used by a supervisor or account manager when they go on site to spot-check work quality. Unlike recurring checklists, audit checklists don't run on a schedule; somebody picks them up and runs them ad-hoc.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Checklists → Audit Checklists
From the list: + Create Checklist opens the create form · row eye button opens read-only Audit Checklist Detail · row pencil button opens Edit Checklist.

Creating an audit checklist

  1. Open Checklists → Audit Checklists from the sidebar.
  2. Click + Create Checklist.
  3. Give it a name and a list of audit items (the things to inspect).
  4. Pick the facilities it can be used at.
  5. Save.

The structure is simpler than recurring checklists — there's no per-item frequency because audits are point-in-time inspections, not schedule-driven.

Both Recurring Checklists and Audit Checklists live in the same underlying checklists table — the difference is the checklist_type field (maintenance vs audit). The two list pages just filter the same data.

Recurring Jobs (history)

A recurring job is one specific instance of a recurring checklist being completed by a cleaner — for example, "Daily Restroom Clean at Oak Tower on 12 Mar 2026". The Recurring Jobs page is the historical log of every completed instance.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Checklists → Recurring Jobs
To open one job: click any row to open Recurring Job Detail (item-by-item record of what was completed / skipped).

What you see

Each row shows the facility, checklist name, scheduled date, completion date, the cleaner who did it, and the completion percentage (how many items they ticked off). The toolbar filters by facility and manager. Click a row to open the job detail page and see exactly which items were completed and which were skipped.

This page is read-only history — it shows completed jobs only. To change a checklist that's about to run in future, edit the underlying template in Recurring Checklists.

Audit Jobs (history)

An audit job is one execution of an audit checklist — somebody went to a site, ran an inspection, and recorded the results. Audit Jobs is the log of every audit that's been completed.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Checklists → Audit Jobs
To open one audit: click any row to open Audit Task Detail (item-by-item findings, photos, notes).

What you see

Same structure as Recurring Jobs — facility, checklist name, scheduled date, completion date, who ran it, completion percentage.

Payroll

Payroll is where you review and approve hours and pay for every cleaner, every pay period (typically monthly). Hours come in automatically from the cleaners' clock-ins and clock-outs in the mobile app — your job is to review them, fix anything that looks wrong, and approve.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Payroll
Drill-down chain: Payroll dashboard → click Review on a row → Payroll Detail (one staff, one period) → click Edit to open Edit Payroll (whole period) or click a single shift's edit button to open Edit Shift (one day).

The payroll dashboard

The page is split into three areas:

  1. Pending Approvals — every active staff member's hours for the current period: pay rate, reported hours, adjustments, payable hours, missed punches, estimated pay, status. Cleaners with a payout request show up at the top.
  2. Payout Requests — staff who have requested an early/partial payout (with amount and hours). Process or dismiss each one.
  3. History — already-approved entries from prior periods.

Stat cards at the top show total payable hours, total pending amount, pending approvals count, processed count for the month.

Approving one staff member's pay

  1. From the Pending Approvals list, click Review next to a row to open the staff member's payroll detail for the period.
  2. The detail page shows day-by-day breakdown — every shift with date, facility, clock-in/clock-out, hours, rate, calculated gross.
  3. If a single shift looks wrong, click the row's edit button to open Edit Shift. Fix hours, rate or notes there and save.
  4. If you need bulk edits across the whole period, click Edit at the top to open Edit Payroll, where every shift is editable in one table.
  5. Once everything looks right, click Approve. The status moves to Approved.
  6. When you've actually paid out, click Mark as Paid. The status moves to Paid.
  7. If you spot something after approval, click Reopen to put the entry back into editable state.

Approving everyone at once

The Approve All button on the dashboard moves every pending entry to Approved in one go. Approve All & Sync does the same and also pushes the period totals out to your accounting / payroll provider via the integration.

Always spot-check unusual entries before Approve All. Look out for: missed punches, very large adjustment hours (positive or negative), or a payable-hours figure that's far above the staff member's typical month. Reviewing one outlier here saves a lot of pain later.

Profitability

Profitability is the financial dashboard — for each month and each facility (or all facilities), it computes Revenue minus Labor cost and Supply cost to get net profit and margin.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Profitability
Filters live in the page header: month dropdown (defaults to current month) and facility dropdown (or leave empty for the portfolio total).

What you see

  • KPI cards — Total Revenue, Total Labor Cost, Total Supply Cost, Net Profit, Total Hours, Avg Hourly Rate, Margin %.
  • Bar chart — facility-by-facility revenue / cost / profit comparison for the selected month.
  • Facility table — every facility with its month-to-date revenue, labor, supply and net profit.

Filters

Pick a month from the dropdown to change the period (defaults to the current month). Pick a facility to drill into one site, or leave empty to see the portfolio total.

Labor cost is computed live from cleaner clock-ins and their hourly rate, so it updates as the month progresses. Supply cost only counts requests in status Approved, Ordered, Delivered or Collected (i.e. anything that's actually been paid for). Revenue comes from the Monthly Revenue tab on each facility — so if a facility doesn't have a revenue figure logged for the month, it shows as zero.

Evaluations

An evaluation is a structured performance review of a single staff member for a single period (typically a month or quarter). Each evaluation is scored against a set of parameters — like Punctuality, Quality of Work, Communication — that you configure once and reuse across all evaluations.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Evaluations
Drill-down chain: Evaluations list → + New Evaluation opens the form · or click any row to open Evaluation Detail → Update Evaluation opens Edit Evaluation.
To configure scoring criteria: use Sidebar → Settings → Evaluation Parameters (see the Settings section).

The evaluations list

Shows every evaluation: staff name, role, the period covered, overall score (out of 10), parameter count, and a flag if the score is below 6 (Needs attention). The list is sorted by most-recently-updated first.

Creating an evaluation

  1. Click + New Evaluation.
  2. Pick the staff member and the period.
  3. The form shows every active evaluation parameter. For each one, give a score (1–10) and optional notes.
  4. You can also click + Add Parameter to add a one-off parameter just for this evaluation (without changing the global parameter list).
  5. Click Save Evaluation. The overall score is computed as the average of all parameter scores.

Editing or reviewing later

Click any row to open the evaluation detail. From there Update Evaluation opens the form again so you can revise scores or notes.

Users

The Users page is the directory of every person with a login to the EFS system — admins, account managers, supervisors and cleaners. Each user belongs to a team and may be assigned to one or more facilities.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Users
To create or edit: Add User opens the new-user popup. Click any row to open the edit popup. Delete appears inside the edit popup.

Adding a user

  1. Open Users from the sidebar.
  2. Click Add User.
  3. Fill in the user's name, email, phone, role (cleaner / supervisor / account manager / admin) and team.
  4. Click Add User to save. The new user receives an invite to log in.

Editing or deactivating a user

Click a row to edit. Save commits changes. Delete soft-deletes the user (sets a delete flag) — historical records like payroll and evaluations stay intact and still reference them.

Documents

Documents is a shared library — policies, training material, safety guides, COSHH sheets, contracts. Anyone with the right permission can browse it.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Documents
Per-row actions: + Upload Document opens the upload popup · View PDF opens the file inline · Delete soft-removes the file.

Uploading a document

  1. Open Documents from the sidebar.
  2. Click + Upload Document.
  3. Pick the file (PDF / image / etc.), give it a name, a category, and any remarks.
  4. Optionally tag account managers and cleaners who should have access.
  5. Click Upload.

Viewing or removing a document

Each row has a View PDF button to open the file inline, and a Delete button to soft-remove it.

Messages

Messages is the team chat — admins, account managers, supervisors and cleaners can hold conversations in one place rather than over WhatsApp / SMS.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Messages
To open a conversation: click an item in the left-hand list — the thread loads on the right. + New Conversation (top of left list) opens a popup to pick participants and start a new thread.

The layout

The page is split-pane: the left side lists every conversation (title, last message preview, unread count, pinned and muted indicators); the right side shows the active conversation thread with messages chronologically and a reply box at the bottom.

Starting a conversation

  1. Click the new-conversation button in the conversations list.
  2. Pick the participants from the team users list.
  3. Give the conversation a title and an opening message.
  4. Click Create.

Replying

Click a conversation to open it. Type in the reply box at the bottom and send. Close archives a conversation if it's no longer needed.

Settings — Evaluation Parameters

Under Settings → Evaluation Parameters you configure the criteria used in every staff evaluation. Each parameter has a name, description, and is scored on a 1–10 scale.

Where to find it: Sidebar → Settings → Evaluation Parameters
Per-row actions: + Add Parameter opens the create popup · Update opens the edit popup · Delete removes the parameter.

Adding a parameter

  1. Open Settings → Evaluation Parameters from the sidebar.
  2. Click + Add Parameter.
  3. Enter the parameter name (e.g. Punctuality) and an optional description.
  4. Click Save.

Editing or removing

Each row has Update (rename / re-describe) and Delete. Changes to parameters apply to new evaluations only — already-saved evaluations keep the parameter scores they were created with, so historical reports stay consistent.

Mobile App — overview

The EFS Mobile phone app is the field-side companion to this web admin portal. The same app supports two roles — it detects who's logging in and shows the right homescreen automatically.

Cleaner mode shows: Tasks · Cleaning Jobs · Supply Requests · Report Issue · Facilities · Documents · Messages · Time Management.
Account Manager mode shows: Attendance · Team Attendance · Supplies · Issues · Tasks · Facility Audits · Facilities · Staff (and Documents).
Both roles see a sticky Attendance card at the top with Clock In / Clock Out controls.

This section walks through every screen the cleaner sees. The Account Manager mode sub-section at the end covers the AM-only views.

Most data the admin reviews on the web portal originates from this mobile app — clock-ins, completed checklists, issue reports, supply requests, photos. The two apps share the same database in real time.

Mobile — Clock In / Clock Out

Every shift starts and ends with a clock-in / clock-out from the mobile homescreen. The app uses the phone's GPS to verify the cleaner is physically at the facility.

Where to find it: the Attendance card sits at the top of the cleaner's homescreen — visible at all times.
Geofence behaviour: after picking a facility, the app shows either "You are well within the facility premises" (clock-in allowed) or "You are X meters away from the chosen site. Kindly come to the facility premises to clock in." A "Click here to know the location" link opens the facility's map.

Clocking in

  1. Open the EFS Mobile app — homescreen shows Welcome, [name] and the Attendance card.
  2. If not yet clocked in, the card shows "Not Clocked In" with a Select the facility you are clocking in to dropdown.
  3. Pick the facility. The app instantly runs a geofence check (compares your phone's GPS against the facility's stored lat / long / radius).
  4. If you're inside the radius, tap Clock In → tap Confirm Clock In. The card flips to "● Clocked In" with the start time shown.
  5. If you're outside the radius, the app blocks clock-in until you move closer to the site.

Clocking out

  1. From the homescreen Attendance card (showing "Clocked in since [time]"), tap Clock Out.
  2. Confirm "Are you sure you want to clock out?" → tap Confirm Clock Out.
  3. Card flips back to "Not Clocked In", ready for the next shift.

Reporting a missed punch

If you forgot to clock in or out (phone dead, lost signal etc.), use Report a Missed Punch on the attendance card — see Time Management below for the full flow.

Mobile — Tasks

Ad-hoc tasks the account manager assigns to a cleaner — different from recurring checklists, which apply to a whole facility on a schedule.

Where to find it: homescreen Tasks tile. Header: "Manage tasks assigned by your account manager."
Filter tabs: All · Pending · Done. Empty state: "No Tasks Assigned — Tasks assigned by your account manager will appear here."

Per-task details

Each task row shows Facility, Due Date, Description, and (if completed) "Completed by [name]".

Completing a task

  1. Tap the row to open Task Detail.
  2. Read the description, look at any attachments.
  3. Tap Mark as Complete. The task moves from Pending to Done.

Mobile — Cleaning Jobs

The recurring checklists for facilities the cleaner is assigned to. Cleaner picks one, ticks items as they go, marks complete.

Where to find it: homescreen Cleaning Jobs tile.
Drill-down chain: Cleaning Jobs list → pick checklist → Fill Checklist → tick items → Complete Cleaning Job.

Filling a checklist

  1. Open Cleaning Jobs from the homescreen.
  2. Pick today's checklist for the facility you've clocked in to. (Or use Create Cleaning Job with Select Facility + Select Checklist dropdowns to start a new instance manually.)
  3. Inside Fill Checklist, tick each item as you complete it. Add notes or photos per item if needed.
  4. Tap Complete Cleaning Job. The instance is marked completed with the completion timestamp.

What admin sees afterwards

The completed instance lands in the web portal's Recurring Jobs (history) with the date, cleaner name, and item-by-item record (which items were ticked, which were skipped, any photos / notes attached).

Mobile — Supply Requests

The cleaner raises supply requests on mobile and confirms when they pick the supplies up. Everything in between (approve, reject, order, deliver) is the account manager's job — see Account Manager mode below for that side.

Where to find it: cleaner's homescreen Supply Requests tile (count badge shows open requests).
What the cleaner can do: raise a new request · view their own past requests · tap Mark Collected on a delivered request to confirm pickup.

Raising a new request (Cleaner)

  1. From homescreen, tap Supply Requests → + New Supply Request.
  2. Pick Select Facility from the dropdown.
  3. For each line item: pick Item Name from the facility's Supply Catalog dropdown (or type a custom item if it's not in the catalog), enter Quantity, optional Notes.
  4. Submit. The request lands as pending for the AM to triage.

Marking a request collected (Cleaner)

Once the AM has marked the request delivered, the cleaner sees a Mark Collected button on Supply Request Detail. Tap it after picking the supplies up — that closes the request as collected and stamps collected_date.

The cleaner cannot approve, reject, order or deliver — those four actions are AM-only and live on a different mobile view (am_supply_request_detail). See Account Manager mode.

Mobile — Report Issue

For broken equipment, safety hazards, access problems, or any on-site issue that needs admin attention.

Where to find it: homescreen Report Issue tile.
To raise: tap + New Issue from the My Issues list.

Reporting an issue

  1. From homescreen, tap Report Issue → opens My Issues.
  2. Tap + New Issue → opens Create Issue form.
  3. Fill:
    • Select Facility (dropdown)
    • Issue Title (text)
    • Issue Type (dropdown — Maintenance, Safety, Access, etc.)
    • Severity (dropdown — low / medium / high / critical)
    • Description (text)
    • Add Photos (camera or gallery)
  4. Submit. Issue lands in admin's Issues list as open.

Tracking your issues

The My Issues list shows every issue you've reported with the current status. Tap any row to see the full thread, admin's resolution notes, and any status updates.

Mobile — Facilities

The list of facilities the cleaner is assigned to — used to look up access notes, shift schedules, contact details and the supply catalog before or during a shift.

Where to find it: homescreen Facilities tile.
To open one: tap any row to open Facility Detail.

What you see for one facility

  • Name, address, geofence radius.
  • Shift schedule for the cleaner (when they're rostered there).
  • Access & Alarms notes (entry codes, key locations).
  • Client contacts (name, phone).
  • Supply Catalog — items typically ordered for this site.
  • Additional info notes.

This data is read-only on mobile. Edits are made by admin on the web portal.

Mobile — Documents

Read-only library of documents the admin has tagged "Visible to Cleaners" — policies, training material, COSHH sheets, safety procedures.

Where to find it: homescreen Documents tile.
To open a document: tap any row to open the inline PDF / file viewer.

Browsing documents

The list shows document name, category, upload date and uploader. Tap any row to open the file in the in-app viewer — works for PDFs, images, and most office formats.

Cleaners cannot upload, edit, or delete documents on mobile — that's an admin-only action on the web portal.

Mobile — Messages

Same team chat as the admin portal's Messages module — every conversation is visible on both apps.

Where to find it: homescreen Messages tile (count badge shows unread messages).
Layout: conversations list → tap a thread to open Conversation Detail.

Starting a new conversation

  1. From the conversations list, tap + New Message.
  2. Pick participants from the team users list.
  3. Type your opening message and send.

Replying

Tap any conversation to open the thread, type in the reply box at the bottom, send. New messages from the other side appear in real time.

Mobile — Time Management

The cleaner's personal timesheet — attendance history, shift details, and missed-punch reporting.

Where to find it: homescreen Time Management tile.
Sub-views: Attendance History · Shift Details · Missed Punches.

Attendance History

Day-by-day log of clock-ins and clock-outs with calculated hours. Use this to verify your hours before payroll cuts off.

Shift Details

The cleaner's recurring weekly schedule across the facilities they're assigned to.

Reporting a missed punch

  1. From Time Management, open Missed Punches → tap + Report Missed Punch.
  2. Fill:
    • Site (dropdown)
    • Actual Clock-In Date & Time
    • Actual Clock-Out Date & Time
    • Reason / Justification (text)
  3. Submit. Admin sees this in Payroll Detail for that staff / period and adjusts the affected shift via Edit Shift.

End-to-end roadmap

Five real-world scenarios — each one a complete flow that crosses the web admin portal and the mobile cleaner / account manager apps. Read these in order to see how the platform fits together end-to-end.

1

Going live with a new facility

Trigger: a new contract is signed and a new site joins the EFS portfolio.
  1. Web · Admin Open Facilities → + Add Facility → fill name, address, geofence (lat / long / radius), cleaning frequency, supply responsibility.
  2. Web · Admin Open the new facility → Shift Schedule tab → + Add Shift with role name, days of week, start / end times.
  3. Web · Admin Staff tab → + Assign Staff for primary + backup cleaners.
  4. Web · Admin Supply Catalog tab → + Add Item for every supply this site routinely orders (becomes the picklist for supply requests).
  5. Web · Admin Client Contacts, Access & Alarms, Additional Info tabs — fill in any on-site information cleaners will need.
  6. Web · Admin Open Checklists → Recurring Checklists → + Create Checklist (or edit an existing one) and assign it to the new facility.
  7. Web · Admin Optional: Audit Checklists → create or assign an audit template for spot-checks.
  8. Mobile · Cleaner Cleaner opens the EFS Mobile app — the new facility now appears in Facilities, the assigned shifts appear in Time Management, and any access notes are visible on the facility's detail page.
  9. Web · Admin Once invoicing starts, log the first month's contracted amount on Facility Detail → Monthly Revenue.
Result: the facility is fully wired up — geofence gates clock-ins, shifts populate the cleaner's roster, recurring checklists drive their daily work, and the revenue line feeds Profitability.
2

A daily cleaning shift

Trigger: a cleaner arrives on site for their scheduled shift.
  1. Mobile · Cleaner Cleaner opens the app — homescreen shows Welcome, [name], an attendance card, and the eight feature tiles (Tasks, Cleaning Jobs, Supply Requests, Issues, Facilities, Documents, Messages, Time Management).
  2. Mobile · Cleaner Taps the attendance card → picks the facility from the dropdown → app runs a geofence check ("You are well within the facility premises" or "X meters away") → taps Clock In.
  3. Mobile · Cleaner Opens Cleaning Jobs → picks today's checklist for the facility (or creates a new one with Create Cleaning Job).
  4. Mobile · Cleaner Inside Fill Checklist, ticks each item as the work is done — can attach notes / photos per item.
  5. Mobile · Cleaner When done, taps Complete Cleaning Job.
  6. Mobile · Cleaner Returns to the homescreen, taps Clock Out.
  7. Web · Admin The completed job lands in Checklists → Recurring Jobs with the date, cleaner, completion %, and item-by-item record visible in detail.
  8. Web · Admin The clock-in / clock-out timestamps land in attendance and roll up into the cleaner's hours in Payroll.
  9. Mobile · AM The account manager can review the day's checklists and team attendance from their AM views (am_facility_checklists, am_team_attendance).
Result: one shift produces three artefacts — a completed Recurring Jobs record, attendance hours feeding payroll, and an updated cleaning history per cleaner.
3

Reporting an on-site issue

Trigger: a cleaner spots broken equipment, a safety hazard, or any problem that needs admin attention.
  1. Mobile · Cleaner On the homescreen, taps Report Issue → opens Create Issue form.
  2. Mobile · Cleaner Picks Facility, enters Issue Title, picks Issue Type, picks Severity (low / medium / high / critical), writes a description, attaches photos. Submits.
  3. Web · Admin Issue lands in Issues with status open; if critical, surfaces in the urgent stat card.
  4. Web · Admin Opens Issue Detail → reviews description and photos → clicks Update Issue → assigns Resolved By → sets status in_progress.
  5. Web · Admin Once the work is done — adds resolution notes → status resolved.
  6. Web · Admin When confirmed and locked → status closed.
  7. Mobile · Cleaner Sees the status updates on My Issues.
  8. Mobile · AM Account manager can also triage issues directly from the am_issues view.
Result: a fully audited paper trail — who reported it, when, photos, who resolved it, with what notes.
4

Supply request lifecycle

Trigger: a cleaner is running out of cleaning solution / PPE / toilet paper at a site.
  1. Mobile · Cleaner Opens Supply Requests → + New Supply Request. Picks facility; for each line item picks Item Name from the facility's Supply Catalog (or types a custom name), enters quantity and notes. Submits → status pending.
  2. Both apps The request lands in Supply Requests on the web admin and in the AM's Supplies tile on mobile (Pending tab). Either side can pick it up — the AM usually does, since they're often on site. Steps 3–5 below can run on either app.
  3. Both apps Approve. AM taps Approve Request on mobile or web admin opens Update Status → picks the Approved card → submits. Status → approved, stamps approved_by + approved_date.
    Mobile · AM only If declining instead, AM taps Reject Request → reason popup → Reject. Status → rejected with rejected_reason. (The web admin doesn't have a reject popup.)
  4. Both apps Order. AM places the actual order with the supplier (off-app), then taps Mark as Ordered on mobile or web admin uses Update Status → Ordered. Status → ordered, stamps ordered_date + ordered_by.
  5. Both apps Deliver. When supplies arrive at the depot, AM taps Mark as Delivered on mobile or web admin uses Update Status → Delivered. Status → delivered, stamps delivered_date + delivered_by.
  6. Mobile · Cleaner Cleaner sees the status update on their Supply Requests list, goes to the depot, picks the supplies up, and taps Mark Collected. Status → collected, stamps collected_date + collected_by. (Web admin can also set Collected via Update Status if needed.)
  7. Web · Admin Total cost flows automatically into Profitability → Supply Cost for the request's facility / month.
  8. Web · Admin Optional: Download on the web detail page → opens Supply Request Invoice for accounting / audit.
Result: supplies delivered and collected, cost captured against the right facility, full chain of custody (pending → approved → ordered → delivered → collected) with the actor stamped on each step. Web admin and AM mobile have full parity on the middle three transitions; only the cleaner-side Mark Collected and the AM-only Reject with reason are app-specific.
5

End-of-month closeout

Trigger: the pay period closes and the admin needs to settle the books.
  1. Web · Admin Opens Profitability → picks last month → reviews each facility's revenue vs labor + supply cost → spots underperformers.
  2. Web · Admin Opens Payroll → reviews Pending Approvals → sorts by missed punches / variance to spot anomalies.
  3. Web · Admin Clicks Review on a row → Payroll Detail → fixes any odd shifts via per-row Edit (opens Edit Shift) or top-level Edit (opens Edit Payroll).
  4. Web · Admin Clicks Approve per row, or Approve All / Approve All & Sync on the dashboard.
  5. Web · Admin Once paid externally, clicks Mark as Paid on each entry — moves status from approved → paid.
  6. Web · Admin Opens Evaluations → + New Evaluation for each staff member for the period → scores parameters (0–10) → submits.
  7. Mobile · AM Account managers can do staff evaluations from the mobile am_staff_evaluation view if they prefer to score on the go.
  8. Web · Admin Updates Monthly Revenue on Facility Detail for any sites whose revenue changed (rate increase, new contract terms, etc.).
Result: the books are settled — payroll out, profitability locked in, performance reviews on file, revenue figures up to date for next month's reporting.

  • Users directory holds each staff's hourly rate (the rate column on payroll comes from there).

Profitability

Lifecycle A computed dashboard, not an entity you create. Inputs come from three places: Monthly Revenue (Facility tab), Attendance × hourly rate (Payroll), approved Supply Requests. Output: per-month, per-facility profit and margin.

2 · Inputs (no create form)

InputSource
RevenueFacility Detail → Monthly Revenue tab
Labor costLive computation: Σ(clock-out − clock-in + adjust) × hourly rate, from attendance + staff
Supply costΣ(total_cost) of supply requests in approved / ordered / delivered / collected

3 · What you see

  • Filters: month dropdown (defaults to current month) · facility dropdown (or All).
  • KPI cards: Total Revenue, Total Labor Cost, Total Supply Cost, Net Profit, Total Hours, Avg Hourly Rate, Margin %.
  • Bar chart: per-facility revenue / cost / profit comparison.
  • Facility table: every facility with its monthly revenue, labor cost, supply cost and net profit.

5 · What you can edit here

Nothing directly — this is a dashboard. To change inputs:

  • Fix revenue: Facility Detail → Monthly Revenue tab → Edit / Add.
  • Fix labor: Payroll → Edit Shift on the affected day.
  • Fix supply: Supply Requests → request detail → edit line items or status.

Mobile companion app

The cleaner / account-manager phone app (EFS Mobile) is the field-side counterpart to this web admin portal. Most data the admin sees actually originates here — clock-ins, completed checklists, issue reports, supply requests, photos. The two apps share the same database; admin sees the rolled-up office view, mobile sees the on-the-ground task view.
